\section{Data Preprocessing \& Feature Engineering - Defenders}

\subsection{Data Cleaning for Defenders}

\textbf{First: Player Filtering}

Players were filtered to include only defenders with at least one appearance. This ensures that the analysis reflects actual defender performance.

\textbf{Second: Dropping Irrelevant Columns}

All columns related to attacking performance were removed, including:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Goals, Goals per match, Headed goals
    \item Goals with right foot, Goals with left foot
    \item Penalties scored, Freekicks scored
    \item Shots, Shots on target, Shooting accuracy \%
    \item Hit woodwork, Big chances missed
    \item Assists, Passes, Passes per match
    \item Big chances created, Crosses, Cross accuracy \%
    \item Through balls, Accurate long balls
\end{itemize}

The reason for removing these columns is that a defender's primary role is defensive, and attacking columns do not reflect their true performance.

\textbf{Third: Feature Engineering}

New features were created to measure defensive performance accurately:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Tackle Success Rate:} Tackle success percentage divided by 100, with handling for cases where no tackles were attempted.
    \[
    \text{tackle\_success\_rate} = \frac{\text{Tackle success \%}}{100} \quad \text{(if Tackles > 0, else NaN)}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Aerial Duel Success:} Aerial battles won divided by total aerial battles (won + lost).
    \[
    \text{aerial\_duel\_success} = \frac{\text{Aerial battles won}}{\text{Aerial battles won} + \text{Aerial battles lost}}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Clearances per Match:} Total clearances divided by number of appearances.
    \[
    \text{clearances\_per\_match} = \frac{\text{Clearances}}{\text{Appearances}}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Duel Success Rate:} Total duels won (both ground and aerial) divided by total duels contested.
    \[
    \text{duel\_success\_rate} = \frac{\text{Duels won}}{\text{Duels won} + \text{Duels lost}}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Errors per Match:} Errors leading to goal divided by number of appearances.
    \[
    \text{errors\_per\_match} = \frac{\text{Errors leading to goal}}{\text{Appearances}}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Defensive Score:} Sum of Tackles, Interceptions, and Blocked shots - a comprehensive measure of defensive performance.
    \[
    \text{defensive\_score} = \text{Tackles} + \text{Interceptions} + \text{Blocked shots}
    \]
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Validation \& Quality Assurance for Defenders}

The following steps were performed to ensure data quality for defenders:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Missing Value Handling:} Missing values were handled using conditional statements (np.where), so if a value is zero or missing, the new feature is not calculated.
    \item \textbf{Percentage Validation:} All categorical percentage distributions were verified to sum to 100\% within rounding tolerance.
    \item \textbf{Descriptive Statistics:} The describe() method was applied to validate data distributions and identify outliers.
    \item \textbf{Data Types:} Numerical columns were verified to ensure correct data types for statistical analysis.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion \& Recommendations}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=lightgray, colframe=primary, arc=5pt, boxrule=1.5pt]
\centering
\textcolor{primary}{\textbf{\Large Summary of Key Findings}}
\end{tcolorbox}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=lightgray, colframe=secondary, arc=5pt, boxrule=1pt]
The comprehensive analysis of defender performance reveals several critical insights that can inform team strategy, player recruitment, and defensive training focus.
\end{tcolorbox}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Aerial Dominance:} Five defenders achieved 100\% aerial duel success, with Virgil van Dijk (74\%) and Harry Maguire (71\%) leading among regular starters.
    
    \item \textbf{Tackling Efficiency:} Phil Bardsley leads all defenders with 310 tackles attempted and an elite 82\% success rate.
    
    \item \textbf{Duel Success:} The average defender wins 50-60\% of duels, with elite performers like Harry Maguire reaching 80\%.
    
    \item \textbf{Defensive Archetypes:} Maguire excels at duels and aerial battles; Bardsley excels at interceptions and clearances.
    
    \item \textbf{Age Profile:} Defenders typically peak in their mid-to-late 20s, later than attacking players.
    
    \item \textbf{Disciplinary Patterns:} Burnley defenders accumulate the most cards (225 yellows, 5 reds), reflecting an aggressive style.
    
    \item \textbf{Two-Way Defenders:} Players like Phil Jagielka, Kyle Walker, and Gary Cahill excel in both tackles and interceptions.
    
    \item \textbf{High-Volume Efficiency:} Top tacklers maintain success rates above 75\%, proving volume and efficiency can coexist.
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=primary!10, colframe=primary, arc=5pt, boxrule=1.5pt]
\textcolor{primary}{\textbf{Final Recommendations:}}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Prioritize defenders with aerial duel success above 70\% for set-piece dominance
    \item Target tacklers with both high volume (250+) and high success rate (75\%+)
    \item Balance defensive profiles: one dominant duel winner + one interceptor/cleaner
    \item Consider age profile; invest in defenders entering their peak years (25-28)
    \item Monitor disciplinary records; high card accumulation risks suspensions
    \item Use the tackles vs interceptions bubble chart to identify complete defenders
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}
