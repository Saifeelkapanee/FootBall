\section{Data Preprocessing \& Feature Engineering - Goalkeepers}

\subsection{Data Cleaning for Goalkeepers}

\textbf{First: Player Filtering}

Players were filtered to include only goalkeepers with at least one appearance. This ensures that the analysis is based on real goalkeepers who have actually played matches.

\textbf{Second: Dropping Irrelevant Columns}

All columns related to outfield player performance were removed, including:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Attacking stats: Goals, Assists, Shots, Shots on target, Shooting accuracy, Crosses, Through balls
    \item Defensive stats: Tackles, Interceptions, Clearances, Duels, Aerial battles, Offsides
    \item Midfield stats: Passes, Big chances created, Accurate long balls
\end{itemize}

The reason for removing these columns is that goalkeepers have completely different responsibilities from outfield players, and these metrics do not apply to their position.

\textbf{Third: Feature Engineering}

New features were created to measure goalkeeper performance accurately:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Save Percentage:} Saves divided by total shots on target (Saves + Goals conceded).
    \[
    \text{save\_percentage} = \frac{\text{Saves}}{\text{Saves} + \text{Goals conceded}}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Clean Sheets per Match:} Clean sheets divided by number of appearances.
    \[
    \text{clean\_sheets\_per\_match} = \frac{\text{Clean sheets}}{\text{Appearances}}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Goals Conceded per Match:} Goals conceded divided by number of appearances.
    \[
    \text{goals\_conceded\_per\_match} = \frac{\text{Goals conceded}}{\text{Appearances}}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Punches \& High Claims per Match:} Total aerial actions (Punches + High Claims) divided by appearances.
    \[
    \text{punches\_high\_claims\_per\_match} = \frac{\text{Punches} + \text{High Claims}}{\text{Appearances}}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Aerial Actions:} Sum of High Claims and Punches.
    \[
    \text{aerial\_actions} = \text{High Claims} + \text{Punches}
    \]
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion \& Recommendations}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=lightgray, colframe=primary, arc=5pt, boxrule=1.5pt]
\centering
\textcolor{primary}{\textbf{\Large Summary of Key Findings}}
\end{tcolorbox}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=lightgray, colframe=secondary, arc=5pt, boxrule=1pt]
The comprehensive analysis of goalkeeper performance reveals several critical insights that can inform team strategy, player recruitment, and goalkeeping development.
\end{tcolorbox}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Shot-Stopping Elite:} Sergio Romero leads with 91\% save percentage, while Joe Hart has the most saves (941) and clean sheets (127).
    
    \item \textbf{Aerial Dominance:} Joe Hart (640 aerial actions) and Lukasz Fabianski (480) dominate in punches and high claims.
    
    \item \textbf{Clean Sheet Efficiency:} Alisson (0.48 CS/match) and Ederson (0.45 CS/match) are the most reliable clean sheet keepers.
    
    \item \textbf{Sweeper-Keeper Role:} Hugo Lloris (257 sweeper clearances) leads modern goalkeepers in sweeping actions.
    
    \item \textbf{Resilience:} Sergio Romero makes approximately 9 saves per goal conceded, an extraordinary ratio.
    
    \item \textbf{Ederson vs Alisson:} Ederson excels at sweeper clearances and clean sheets; Alisson makes more saves per match.
    
    \item \textbf{Penalty Saving:} Many goalkeepers (including Alisson, Ederson, De Gea) have 100\% penalty save rates on penalties faced.
    
    \item \textbf{Handling Quality:} The catch vs punch analysis helps identify goalkeepers with secure vs aggressive handling styles.
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=primary!10, colframe=primary, arc=5pt, boxrule=1.5pt]
\textcolor{primary}{\textbf{Final Recommendations:}}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Target goalkeepers with save percentage above 70\% for consistent shot-stopping
    \item Prioritize aerial dominance (high punches + high claims) for teams facing many crosses
    \item Consider sweeper-clearance ability for teams playing high defensive lines
    \item Use saves-per-goal ratio to identify undervalued shot-stoppers
    \item Balance catching vs punching tendencies based on defensive system
    \item Clean sheet efficiency is the most reliable predictor of defensive stability
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}
